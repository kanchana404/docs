# Customer Journeys & Data Drill-Downs

At the end of the day, none of this is a black box. If you ever have a question about how we’re getting your data or how we’re showing it to you, you can always dig into every single piece of information. Here is how to drill into your customer journeys and understand exactly how your leads are interacting with your brand.

### Accessing the Journey

There are two main ways to find your customer data:

1. **From the Attribution Table:** Click on any name, email, or phone number in your reports.
2. **From the Contacts Tab:** Search for a specific contact and click their name to open their profile.

Both of these will open the same detailed view, showing you the full contact details on the left and the chronological **Customer Journey** on the right.

### Managing the Relationship

Inside the contact profile, you can communicate with your leads directly:

- **Messaging:** Send messages via HighLevel, our own phone provider, or even iMessage.
- **Email:** Send emails directly from the dashboard.
- **Contact Info:** Update tags, business info, or location data manually on the left-hand side.

### Deep Diving into Session Data

In the Customer Journey panel, you’ll see every page view and conversion event. For each one, you can click **View Data** to see the "under the hood" details:

- **Page View Details:** See the full URL, time spent on page, and scroll depth.
- **URL Parameters:** We break out all the UTM parameters (source, medium, campaign, etc.) so you can see exactly which ad or link brought them there.
- **Technical Details:** View the device type, browser, IP address, and cookie ID.

### Troubleshooting with Metadata

If you’re seeing something that doesn't look right—like a purchase showing up as $0—you can use the **Webhook Metadata** to find the answer.

By clicking **View Data** on a conversion event, you can see the raw payload sent from your source platform (like HighLevel or Shopify). This tells you exactly what data we received. Often, if a value is missing or incorrect, it’s because that information wasn't sent over in the original webhook, and you can spot that immediately in the metadata.

<Tip>**Pro Tip:** If you have Meta CAPI turned on, the conversion data view will also confirm whether that specific event was successfully sent back to Meta for ad optimization.</Tip>

### Integration Tabs

As you connect more platforms, your Customer Journey view gets even more powerful. You’ll see additional tabs for:

- **Stripe/Whop:** View MRR, total payments, and active customer status.
- **Fathom:** Access session recordings to watch exactly how the user navigated your site.
- **Shopify:** View specific order details and purchase history.

Understanding the customer journey is the best way to see the "real-world" impact of your ads—like when a customer clicks an ad, leaves, then searches your brand on Google later that day to finally book a call. That’s the level of visibility we want to give you.
