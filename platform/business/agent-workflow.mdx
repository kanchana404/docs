## Introduction to the Agent Workflow Interface

Welcome to the Agent Workflow interface within Agent Hub. This is your primary workspace for designing and building the conversational workflows for your AI agents across various communication channels. This guide will walk you through the user interface and the fundamental features available to help you get started. For more in-depth tutorials on building complete agent workflows, please refer to our full training series.

## Navigating the Main Interface

The Agent Workflow interface is designed to be intuitive and efficient, allowing you to seamlessly create and manage your AI agent's conversations.

1. **Channel Selection**: At the top left of your screen, you can select the specific communication channel you want to build a flow for, such as:
   - iMessage & SMS
   - Instagram
   - Facebook
   - Email
   - WhatsApp
2. **AI Activation Toggle**: Next to the channel selection, you'll find a toggle switch to easily turn your AI agent on or off for the selected channel.
3. **Return to Agent Hub**: The "Agent Hub" button allows you to quickly navigate back to the main dashboard.

## Configuring Your Agent's Settings

The settings panel is where you can fine-tune the behavior and personality of your AI agent.

1. **Accessing Settings**: Click the **gear icon** located in the top navigation bar to open the settings menu.
2. **Key Settings**: Within this panel, you can configure various aspects of your agent, including:
   - **Conversation Settings**: Define the purpose and context of the conversation.
   - **Agent Voice**: Customize the agent's personality and tone.
   - **Follow-Up Sequences**: Create and manage how the agent follows up with contacts.
   - **And much more!**

## Managing Your Workflows

The ability to import and export your agent flows allows for easy sharing, backup, and duplication of your work.

1. **Exporting a Flow**:
   - Click the **Export Flow** icon (upward arrow) in the top right corner.
   - This will download the current workflow as a JSON file to your computer.
   - This feature is perfect for saving your progress, sharing with others, or moving a flow to a different account.
2. **Importing a Flow**:
   - Click the **Import Flow** icon (downward arrow) in the top right corner.
   - Select the JSON file of the flow you wish to import from your computer.
   - The imported workflow will then populate on your canvas.

## Testing and Building Your Flow

The canvas is your main building area, and the testing feature allows you to see your agent in action.

1. **Adding Nodes**:
   - Click the **plus icon** on the left-hand side to open the "Add Workflow Node" panel.
   - You can either **click** on a node to add it to the canvas or **drag and drop** it to a specific location.
2. **Testing Your Flow**:
   - Click the **Test Flow** button at the bottom of the screen.
   - This opens a chat interface where you can interact with your agent as if you were a user.
   - The **Session Logs** on the left of the chat show the behind-the-scenes actions of the agent, which is invaluable for debugging.

## Interface Controls and Shortcuts

To help you build more efficiently, the interface includes several helpful controls and keyboard shortcuts.

- **Dark Mode**: In the top right corner, you can toggle between light and dark modes to suit your preference.
- **Undo/Redo**: In the bottom left corner, you will find buttons to undo and redo your previous actions.
- **Keyboard Shortcuts**: Click the **question mark icon** in the bottom left to view a full list of keyboard shortcuts for actions like selecting multiple nodes, copying, and pasting.
  - **Multi-select with mouse**: Hold **Shift** and drag your mouse to select multiple nodes.
  - **Multi-select with clicks**: Hold **Shift** and click on individual nodes to select them.

## Share Test Link

**Share Test Link** allows you to easily share a live testing environment for your AI Agent with clients or team members, without requiring them to log in to the platform.

### 1. Accessing the Share Test Link

1. Navigate to your desired workflow canvas.
2. Ensure the **Agent Status Toggle** for the selected channel (e.g., iMessage & SMS) is **ON** and configured with a provider, as a link cannot be shared for an inactive objective.
3. Click the **Test Link** button in the top-right corner of the interface.
4. A modal titled **Share Test Link** will appear.

### 2. Sharing the Test Link

The modal contains a unique public link for testing your agent.

#### Step-by-Step:

1. Click the **Copy Link** button. The link is now copied to your clipboard.
2. Send this link to anyone you wish to test the agent (clients, team members, or other stakeholders).

#### Key Features of the Test Link:

- **Public Access:** This link is public and **does not require a login**, making it ideal for sharing with clients or team members for approval and feedback.
- **Live Testing:** When the recipient opens the link, they will enter a live test session.
- **Session Logs:** The test session displays the **Session Logs** in real-time, allowing both you and the tester to see the AI's internal events, received messages, system activities (like milestone changes), and the AI's responses.
  - **Tool Call Data:** Testers can even view the raw tool call data to see what information the AI is using to process a message.
- **New Session:** The recipient can click **New Session** to clear the conversation and start a fresh test.

### 3. Starting a Test Session

When the recipient opens the link, they will be prompted to start the test.

1. A pre-session modal will display the:
   - **Agent** name.
   - **Objective** (e.g., iMessage & SMS).
   - **Channel** (e.g., SMS).
   - **Test Contact** (typically your own contact name).
2. Click **Start Test Session**.
3. The chat interface will load, and the first messages of your workflow will be initiated, allowing the user to interact with the agent immediately and test the flow logic live.
