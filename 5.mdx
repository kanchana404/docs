### **What Are URL Parameters?**

_Simply put, url parameters are data points you add inside of a URL._

_Lets say your URL was:_

> _www.example.com/landingpage?utm\\\_source=Instagram&utm\\\_campaign=happy_

Everything before the "?" is your domain, and everything after is considered a URL parameter.

The data we pull from this example is:

- URL: <u>www.example.com/landingpage</u>
- URL Parameters:
  - UTM Source = Instagram
  - UTM Campaign = Happy

URL parameters are ESSENTIAL to track where people are coming from.

You can add URL parameters to your ads (videos below) or custom URL parameters if you wish to track things like email campaigns, YouTube Organic tracking, etc.

    # URL Parameters for Meta Ads

    Setting up URL parameters for your Meta ads is a straightforward process, but it’s the most critical step for accurate attribution. Here is how to get them installed and verified.

    ### 1. Adding Parameters to a Single Ad

    To get started, navigate to the **Ad** level in your Meta Ads Manager.

    1. Scroll down to the **Tracking** section.
    2. Locate the **URL parameters** field.
    3. Copy the specific parameter string provided in your dashboard and paste it directly into this box.

    ### 2. Bulk Editing Multiple Ads

    If you have dozens of ads to update, you don't need to do them one by one.

    1. Select all the ads you want to update from the Ads Manager list.
    2. Click **Edit**.
    3. Scroll down to the **Tracking** section and paste your URL parameters into the field.
    4. Hit **Publish**.

    <Warning>
      **Note on Publishing:** When you hit publish, Meta will momentarily pause your ads while they review the changes. This is a standard small change, so ads are typically back up and running within 5 to 15 minutes.
    </Warning>

    ### 3. Verifying Your Setup

    It's important to keep an eye on your tracking status to ensure everything is configured properly. There are two main ways to do this:

    ### In Meta Ads Manager

    I recommend customizing your columns in Ads Manager to include **URL parameters**. This allows you to visibly confirm at a glance which ads have the tracking code installed and which do not.

    ### In Your Attribution Dashboard

    Inside your tracking table, look for the **Tracking** column. This acts as a "root check" for your setup:

    - **Green Checkmark:** You’re good to go! The URL parameters are configured properly.
    - **Red Triangle:** There is an error in the string.
    - **Red X:** Parameters are missing entirely.

    <Tip>
      If you see anything other than a green checkmark, simply click the icon to see exactly what is missing or misconfigured so you can fix it quickly.
    </Tip>

    ```jsx
    utm_source=Meta+Ads&utm_medium={{adset.name}}&utm_campaign={{campaign.name}}&utm_content={{ad.name}}&campaign_id={{campaign.id}}&medium_id={{adset.id}}&content_id={{ad.id}}
    ```

    > NOTE: we are backwards compatible with Hyros URL Parameters

    # URL Parameters for Google Ads

    Adding URL parameters to your Google Ads account is quite simple. Whether you're updating your existing campaigns or starting a fresh one, here’s how to get your tracking set up.

    ### For Existing Campaigns

    If you already have Google ads running, follow these steps to bulk update your tracking:

    1. Navigate to **Campaigns** in your Google Ads dashboard.
    2. Click the **Settings** tab.
    3. Select all the campaigns you want to track.
    4. Click the **Edit** button and select **Change tracking templates**.
    5. Copy and paste the URL parameters below in your dashboard into the **New tracking template** field.
    6. Hit **Apply**.

    <Note>
      ```text wrap
      {lpurl}?campaign_id={campaignid}&medium_id={adgroupid}&ad_id={creative}&utm_keyword={keyword}
      ```
    </Note>

    ### For New Campaigns

    If you’re creating a new campaign from scratch, you can set your tracking template during the setup process:

    1. Start your new campaign creation.
    2. Navigate to the **Campaign settings** section.
    3. Scroll to the bottom, click **More settings**, and then select **Campaign URL options**.
    4. Paste your tracking code into the **Tracking template** field.
    5. Click **Next** and continue with your campaign build—away you go!

    <Tip>
      Setting your tracking at the campaign level ensures that all ads within that campaign automatically inherit the correct URL parameters, saving you time and preventing manual errors.
    </Tip>

    ## Overview

    Cortana's **Attribution Builder** lets you tag any organic traffic source — YouTube, Instagram, email, or anything else — so you can see exactly which content drives opt-ins, calls, and purchases inside your attribution table.

    You don't _need_ Cortana to build UTM links, but the builder makes it dead simple and keeps your naming consistent.

    <Note>
      The page you're sending traffic to **must have the Cortana pixel installed**. Without it, page views and visitor tracking won't work — none of this data will show up.
    </Note>

    ## Accessing the Attribution Builder

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open URL Builder">
        Navigate to **Ad Tracking** → **URL Builder**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Switch to the Attribution tab">
        Click the **Attribution Builder** tab at the top of the URL Builder page.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ## Understanding UTM Dimensions

    Think of UTMs like **nested folders** that organize where your traffic came from. Each layer gets more specific:

    | Dimension | What it represents | Example |
    | --- | --- | --- |
    | **Source** | The platform | `YouTube Organic`, `Instagram Organic`, `Email` |
    | **Campaign** | The channel or project | `mateis-channel`, `weekly-newsletter` |
    | **Medium** | The specific piece of content | `awesome-video-1` |
    | **Ad** | The exact placement or link | `link-in-bio`, `pinned-comment` |

    <Tip>
      There's no single "perfect" structure — just think top-down. What's the broadest category? What's underneath it? Keep nesting until you've captured enough detail to be useful.
    </Tip>

    ## Building a Tracking URL

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Enter your destination URL">
        Paste the page URL you're sending traffic to (e.g. `mylandingpage.com/vsl`). Optionally give it a title for your own reference.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add UTM dimensions">
        Fill in the dimensions from top to bottom:

        - **Source** → `YouTube Organic`
        - **Campaign** → `mateis-channel`
        - **Medium** → `awesome-video-1`
        - **Ad** → `awesome-video-1-link-in-bio`

        Click **Create** after entering each value.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Copy the generated URL">
        The builder appends query parameters to your base URL automatically:

        ```text
        mylandingpage.com/vsl?utm_source=YouTube+Organic&utm_medium=awesome-video-1&utm_campaign=mateis-channel&utm_content=awesome-video-1-link-in-bio
        ```

        Everything after the `?` is tracking data — the visitor still sees the same page.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Place the link">
        Paste the generated URL wherever the traffic will come from (link in bio, video description, email CTA, etc.).
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    ## How the Data Shows Up

    Once someone clicks your tagged link, Cortana tracks their full journey. Inside the **Attribution Table** you can:

    - Filter by **source** (e.g. `YouTube Organic`) to see all traffic from that platform.
    - Drill into **campaign** to compare channels or projects.
    - Go deeper into **medium** and **ad** to see which specific video or link placement drove the conversion.

    If you left a dimension empty, the table will display **"No campaign provided"** (or the equivalent for that level) — which is totally fine if you don't need that layer of detail.

    ## Keeping It Simple (Email Example)

    Not every source needs four layers. For email, if you just want to know "this came from email" without breaking it down further, you can set **all four dimensions** to `email`:

    | Dimension | Value |
    | --- | --- |
    | Source | `email` |
    | Campaign | `email` |
    | Medium | `email` |
    | Ad | `email` |

    This keeps your attribution table clean without creating unnecessary granularity.

    <Info>
      If you only fill in **Source** and leave the rest blank, the attribution table will show the source correctly but display "no campaign provided" when you try to drill down. Fill all four with the same value if you want a clean look without extra detail.
    </Info>
