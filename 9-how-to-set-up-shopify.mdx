# Shopify Integration & Conversion Tracking

Alright, guys, Shopify has a bit of a specific setup process to ensure we track every single visit and purchase on your site. I’m going to walk you through the end-to-end process, from installing the pixel to mapping your conversion webhooks.

### Step 1: Connect Your Store

First, head over to the **Integrations** tab and find **Shopify**.

1. Click **Connect**.
2. Enter your **Shopify Store URL** (e.g., admin.shopify.com/store/your-store-name).
3. Click **Continue**.

### Step 2: Install the Header Snippet

The system will generate a script snippet. We need to add this to your theme’s code:

1. Copy the snippet provided in the dashboard.
2. Click **Theme Editor** (or navigate to **Online Store > Themes > Edit Code** in Shopify).
3. Open the theme.liquid file.
4. Search (Cmd/Ctrl + F) for `</head>`.
5. Paste your snippet on a new line just **before** the `</head>` tag.
6. Hit **Save**, then return to our dashboard and click **I pasted the snippet**.

### Step 3: Set Up the Custom Pixel

Next, we’ll use Shopify’s "Customer Events" to track deep data like cart additions and checkouts:

1. Copy the **Pixel Code** provided in the second step.
2. Click the **Events** button in the dashboard to jump to the right spot in Shopify.
3. Click **Add custom pixel**, give it a name (e.g., "Our Pixel"), and click **Add pixel**.
4. Paste the code into the editor.
5. **Customer Privacy Settings:**
    - **Permission:** Select **Not required**.
    - **Data sale:** Select **Data collected does not qualify as data sale**.
6. Click **Save**, then click **Connect** at the top.
7. Return to the dashboard and click **Verify your site**. This will open your store in a new tab to confirm the pixel is firing.

### Step 4: Map Your Conversions (Webhooks)

Now that the tracking is live, we need to map specific events like purchases back to the system.

1. Go to **Conversion Setup** and select the event you want to track (e.g., **Order Payment** or **Purchase Paid**).
2. Click **Add Source**, select **Shopify**, and choose the corresponding **Shopify Event**.
3. Copy the **Webhook URL** provided.
4. Click **Open Shopify Webhooks** (or go to **Shopify Settings > Notifications > Webhooks**).
5. Click **Create Webhook**:
    - **Event:** Select the matching event (e.g., "Order payment").
    - **Format:** JSON.
    - **URL:** Paste the Webhook URL you copied.
    - **API Version:** October 2025 (or the latest recommended).
6. Click **Save**.

<Tip>
**Verify Your Webhook:** After saving, click the three dots next to your new webhook in Shopify and hit **Send test notification**. You should see a "Received" status with a green checkmark in our dashboard.
</Tip>

### Standard Shopify Events

Most users will want to map these core events to get the best attribution data:

- **View Product:** Triggered when a customer views a specific product page.
- **Add to Cart:** Triggered when an item is added to the shopping bag.
- **Initiate Checkout:** Triggered when the customer reaches the first stage of the checkout.
- **Order Payment:** This is the big one—it triggers once the payment is successfully processed.

And that’s it! You now have a complete, end-to-end Shopify setup for your attribution. If you run into any issues, just reach out to the support team.
