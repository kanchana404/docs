# Contact Identity Resolution

Alright, now that we've got the pixel on the website, we need to handle **Contact Identity Resolution**. This basically means we want to know exactly *who* is visiting—not just that a "visitor" landed, but their name, email, and phone number. We want as much data on them as humanly possible.

### How it Works

Our pixel is incredibly powerful. Once it's on your site, it starts tracking page views, time on site, scroll depth, and even device info in real-time. But for attribution to really work, we need that contact info.

There are two ways this happens depending on your form setup:

### 1. Native Forms (The Easy Way)

If you are using a form that is native to your website builder (like a GoHighLevel form on a GoHighLevel page), you're golden.

<Card title="Automatic Tracking" icon="bolt">
  Our pixel automatically picks up the name, email, and phone number directly from the form fields. You don't need to do any extra work—it just works.
</Card>

### 2. Embedded Forms & iFrames

If you're using something like **Typeform**, **Jotform**, or **Tally** embedded on your page, it's a bit different. These are "iFrame embeds," meaning the form is actually living on a different server. Because of that, our pixel can't "see" inside the form to grab the data while the user is typing.

To fix this, we use **Redirect URLs** to pass that data back to our pixel.

## Setting Up Redirects for iFrames

If you're using an embedded form, follow these steps to make sure we don't miss any contact data:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set a Redirect URL">
    In your form builder (like Typeform), go to the **Endings** or **Thank You Page** settings and choose **Redirect to URL**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add URL Parameters">
    You'll want to redirect users to your "Thank You" page, but you need to append the data to the end of the URL using a `?`. This allows our pixel to pick up the info from the address bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Map the Fields">
    Your redirect URL should look something like this:

```text
    https://yourwebsite.com/thank-you?email={email}&phone={phone}&firstname={firstname}&lastname={lastname}
```

    *Note: Use the "Insert Variable" feature in your form builder to make sure the brackets pull the actual user data.*
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  **Important:** For this to work, our pixel must be installed on **both** the page where the form is embedded AND the page you are redirecting them to.
</Note>

## Why This Matters

Without knowing the name, email, and phone number, we can show you page visits, but we can't tie those visits to a specific customer journey. Once the pixel identifies the user—either through a native form or a redirect URL—it syncs all that "anonymous" data into a real contact profile.

It's so good at tracking that even if you try to hide in an Incognito tab on Safari, we're probably still going to find you.
